Paneer

Paneer is a soft, fresh Indian cheese made by curdling milk with an acidic agent like lemon juice or vinegar. It is non-melting, making it ideal for various dishes like Paneer Butter Masala and Palak Paneer. Rich in protein and calcium, itâ€™s a staple in vegetarian cuisine.

ingredients for paneer

  • Full-fat milk (1 liter)
  • Lemon juice or vinegar (2-3 tablespoons)
  • Water (optional, to dilute lemon juice/vinegar)
  • Cheesecloth or muslin cloth
  • Ice cubes (optional, for firm texture)

steps to make paneer

  1. Boil the milk in a large pot over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent it from sticking.
  2. Dilute the lemon juice or vinegar with a little water (optional) to reduce the tanginess.
  3. Once the milk starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and slowly add the diluted lemon juice or vinegar, one tablespoon at a time.
  4. Stir gently as the milk curdles and separates into curds (solid) and whey (liquid).
  5. Once the whey is clear and greenish, turn off the heat immediately.
  6. Place a cheesecloth over a colander and pour the curdled milk through it to separate the curds from the whey
  7. Rinse the curds under cold water to remove the lemon/vinegar taste and cool them.
  8. Gather the ends of the cheesecloth and squeeze out excess water
  9. Place the wrapped curds on a flat surface, weigh them down with a heavy object, and let them set for 30 minutes to 1 hour
  10. Once firm, unwrap the paneer and cut it into desired shapes or sizes
