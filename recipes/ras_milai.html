ras milai

Rasmalai is a creamy Indian dessert made with soft paneer balls soaked in thickened milk, flavored with cardamom, saffron, and garnished with nuts like pistachios and almonds. It is served chilled and is a popular festive treat.

ingredients for ras milai

  • Full-fat milk (for paneer) – 1 liter
  • Lemon juice or vinegar – 2-3 tablespoons
  • Water – for diluting lemon juice/vinegar
  • Sugar – 1 cup
  • Water (for sugar syrup) – 4 cups
  • Full-fat milk (for rabri) – 1 liter
  • Cardamom powder – 1 teaspoon
  • Saffron strands – a pinch (optional)
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) – for garnish
  • Rose water – 1 teaspoon (optional)

steps to make ras milai

  1. Boil 1 liter of milk and curdle it using diluted lemon juice or vinegar as described in the paneer recipe above.
  2. Rinse the curds with cold water to remove the acidic taste.
  3. Knead the paneer for 5-7 minutes into a smooth, soft dough.
  4. Divide the dough into small portions and shape into flat discs (about 1-inch diameter).
  5. In a large pot, boil 4 cups of water with 1 cup sugar until fully dissolved.
  6. Gently drop the paneer discs into the boiling sugar syrup, cover the pot, and cook for 10-12 minutes on medium heat.
  7. The discs will double in size. Remove from the syrup and set aside.
  8. In another pot, boil 1 liter of milk over medium heat until it reduces to half its volume, stirring occasionally.
  9. Add cardamom powder, saffron strands, and sugar (to taste) to the reduced milk.
  10. Simmer for 5-7 minutes, then remove from heat. Add rose water (optional).
  11. Gently squeeze excess syrup from the paneer discs and place them in the warm rabri.
  12. Allow the rasmalai to soak in the rabri for at least 1-2 hours
  13. Garnish with chopped nuts and chill in the refrigerator before serving.
